Australia Before Colonisation

Before European settlement, Australia was home to one of the world’s oldest continuous human civilisations. Indigenous Australians lived across the continent for more than 60,000 years, developing sustainable lifestyles, deep environmental knowledge, and complex social systems.

The First Australians

The First Nations peoples arrived in Australia during the last Ice Age. Over thousands of generations, they adapted to diverse environments including deserts, coastlines, forests, and grasslands.

Communities were governed by kinship systems, cultural law, and strong spiritual connections to land and water.

Life in Australia 60,000 Years Ago

Daily life involved hunting, fishing, gathering, and advanced land management techniques such as controlled burning. These practices supported biodiversity and food security.

Archaeological evidence reveals stone tools, rock art, shelters, and long-distance trade networks across Australia.

Why This History Matters

Understanding Australia before colonisation provides a more accurate view of the nation’s past and recognises the resilience, innovation, and cultural depth of Indigenous Australians.

This knowledge remains a vital part of Australia’s identity today.
